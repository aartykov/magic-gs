MAGIC-GS: Monocular Articulated GenerIC object reconstruction with Gaussian Splatting

Arslan Artykov, Antoine Guédon, Clémentin Boittiaux, Van Nguyen Nguyen, Vincent Lepetit
LIGM, Ecole des Ponts, Univ Gustave Eiffel, CNRS

We introduce a method that recovers 3D geometry, appearance, and motion of generic articulated objects from a monocular video.

Abstract

3D semantic understanding of objects is a critical topic in computer vision, for robotics, or the automatic creation of digital twins for example. While ubiquitous, articulated objects remain a challenge, due to the various possible combinations of articulations and the high degrees of freedom. In this paper, we introduce an approach able to recover the geometry of articulated objects and an interpretable parameterization of its joints from a single video. We formalize the problem using Gaussian Splatting, optimizing over the Gaussians but also over the different articulated parts and the Gaussian-part assignments. This however results in a difficult optimization problem, and we show how to solve it efficiently. Our method provides an accurate segmentation of the different parts and accurate estimates of their motions. By contrast with previous works, we can work with videos of multi-part objects under motion, without requiring training data.

Method Overview

Given an RGB-D video with corresponding time steps, we first initialize 3D gaussian centers by back-projecting the video. Then, we optimize the deformation field that represents individual transformation of each Gaussian from the canonical space to the corresponding time step. Finally, we detect inlier set of gaussians according to their trajectory and refine the part segmentation and joint parameters by optimizing our original formalization.
